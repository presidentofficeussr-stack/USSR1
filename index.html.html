About the USSR Government

Constitutional Foundation

The Soviet Constitution

The USSR operates under the Stalin Constitution of 1936, superseded by the Constitution of 1977 (Brezhnev Constitution), establishing a socialist state governed by the people through soviets at all levels. The Communist Party of the Soviet Union (CPSU) is the leading and guiding force of Soviet society.

Supreme Legislative Body

Supreme Soviet of the USSR

The highest organ of state power, consisting of two equal chambers: the Soviet of the Union (representing equal populations) and the Soviet of Nationalities (representing all national and territorial units). Convenes twice yearly; elects the Presidium to act between sessions.

Executive Administration

Council of Ministers

The highest executive and administrative organ of state power, formed by the Supreme Soviet. Headed by the Chairman (Premier), the Council directs the economy, implements legislation, oversees ministries, and coordinates the work of all state bodies across the Union.

Soviet Armed Forces

Ground Forces

Soviet Army (Советская Армия)

Largest component of the armed forces with approximately 1.8 million active personnel organized into military districts. Commands include Western TVD, Southwestern TVD, and Far Eastern TVD. Equipped with T-72/T-80 main battle tanks, BMP infantry vehicles, and extensive artillery.

Strategic Forces

Strategic Rocket Forces

Independent service branch controlling all land-based intercontinental and intermediate-range ballistic missiles. Maintains approximately 1,400 ICBMs including SS-18 Satan, SS-19 Stiletto, and SS-25 Sickle. Commanded by Chief Marshal of Artillery.

Air Power

Soviet Air Forces (VVS)

Operates over 9,000 combat aircraft including MiG-23, MiG-29, Su-27, and Tu-95 Bear strategic bomber. The Air Defence Forces (PVO) maintain separate interceptor regiments and surface-to-air missile batteries throughout USSR territory.

Naval Power

Soviet Navy (VMF)

Four fleets: Northern, Pacific, Baltic, and Black Sea. Submarine force numbers over 300 boats including Typhoon-class SSBNs carrying SS-N-20 SLBMs. Surface fleet includes Kiev-class aircraft carriers, Kirov-class battlecruisers, and Slava-class cruisers.

Space & Technology

Space Programme (Roscosmos/VKS)

Leading the world in space exploration. Launched first satellite Sputnik (1957), first human in space Yuri Gagarin (1961), first space station Salyut. Operates Baikonur Cosmodrome in Kazakhstan. Current missions include Mir space station program.

Internal Troops

KGB Border Guards

Approximately 220,000-strong border protection force guarding 62,000 km of Soviet borders. Equipped with patrol vessels, helicopters, and guard dogs. Operates under KGB command with border crossings, customs, and maritime patrol responsibilities.

State Committees & Departments

Planning & Economy

Gosplan — State Planning Committee

The supreme economic planning body of the USSR. Prepares Five-Year and annual plans for all sectors. Sets output targets, resource allocation quotas, and investment priorities. Chairman: Nikolai Baibakov (1965–1985).

Science & Technology

Academy of Sciences (AN SSSR)

The highest scientific institution of the USSR with over 300 research institutes and 60,000 scientists. Oversees all fundamental research. Divisions include: Physics, Chemistry, Mathematics, Biology, Earth Sciences, Social Sciences. President: Anatoly Alexandrov.

Atomic Energy

Minsredmash (Ministry of Medium Machine Building)

Covers the entire Soviet nuclear weapons and nuclear energy complex. Oversees design bureaus, uranium enrichment, weapons assembly, and the civilian nuclear power program including the RBMK and VVER reactor types.

Statistics

TsSU — Central Statistical Administration

Collects and publishes all official national statistics. Produces the annual "National Economy of the USSR" yearbook. Tracks industrial output, population, trade, agriculture, and social indicators. Reports directly to Council of Ministers.

Supply

Gossnab — Material-Technical Supply

Responsible for distribution and allocation of all producer goods (machinery, materials, equipment) throughout the Soviet economy. Manages over 2,000 commodity groups across all enterprises and coordinates inter-enterprise supply contracts.

Prices

Goskomtsen — State Committee on Prices

Sets and regulates all official prices in the Soviet economy for both producer and consumer goods. Maintains price stability as mandated by the Plan. Manages a unified price list of over 20 million commodity prices and tariffs.

Workers' Rights & Benefits
